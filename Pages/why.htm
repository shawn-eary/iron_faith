Why Do an Ironman?

I'm just getting started so you're probably wondering why a burnt out computer programmer is bothering to write this eBook. There isn't much here and I'm certainly no athletic professional, but there is a much bigger question at stake.

Why Should You do a full Ironman?

Why should you get up early in the morning and train for the next few years so you can: swim 2.4 miles ride a bike 112-miles and then run a 26.22 mile marathon thereby beating your body to exhaustion?

It's because Ironman is about change. This book is dedicated to my daughter Faith because Ironman is as much a spiritual journey as it is a physical journey. Many Ironman finishers will tell you the Ironman experience is a life changing event. I feel very strongly that once you ethically cross the Ironman finish line, you will never be the same again.

Some people are going to think you are crazy, stupid and/or would be just plain wasting your time to do something that is both seemingly mundane and impossible at the same time. On the one hand, the idea of wasting nearly every ounce of the valuable free time the Lord has given you so you can exercise seems selfish; but on the other, you might be surprised how empowered in the Lord you feel when you have learned to swim 2.4 maybe even 4 or 5 miles in a lake when you could barely swim 25 yards to start. Also, if you are anything like me and other Ironman competitors, at some point during your training or the event, you will wind up praying. The "Ironman Process" is a transformation where you take off your old self and open up to meditation, prayer and self-discipline. Ironman is a journey of Faith.

Besides being a great way to reunite with the Lord as sister Maddona Budder does. Ironman is a great way to heal from anxiety, depression and prescription/ illegal drug dependencies. You take Ironman seriously, so as you progress through this process, you will likely find yourself gradually needing less prescription medications and your sleeping patterns will probably get better. While the rest of the world is sitting around on their rears binging on Netflix and consuming an excess of processed foods, you will be out in the heat jogging your final miles of that day showing everyone what you are really made of. Through Ironman, you will show the world that are are not a quitter and that you don't change course lightly. While I can't overemphasize how much Faith you will use and develop to cross the finish line, Ironman isn't only a journey of Faith. For some, Ironman can be a "Reset Button"

I write to you in this style because I'm confident in your ability to finish a full Ironman. I look forward to sharing my Ironman experience and training thoughts with you in the days ahead, but there is a very small portion of people that do need to take pause before embarking on something this monumental and potentially dangerous. While most people still have far more "juice" in their bodies than they can possibly imagine, it wouldn't hurt to discuss your training thoughts with a general physician. While Ironman can bring great healing for most, it can actually cause great harm to others. You must be aware of the reckless vehicle drivers, elements of nature, cramps and water creatures you will encounter. You will also need to be aware of the state of your body before you begin training. If you can't swim 25 years in a pool without getting out of breath, then don't go out immediately and try open water swimming. Also, not only will you need to discuss your plans with your doctor and get the okay to start your training regimen, but you will need to gradually get off any medications you may be taking.

I want you to finish your full Ironman, but I must be clear. I am a computer programmer by trade. I am not a physician, nutritionist or athletic trainer. I completed my first and possibly only Ironman in Florida and have learned at lot along the way. What I will say in this eBook will come with absolutely no warranty. I am not qualified to give you any kind of coaching or medical advice. Should the Lord allow me to finish, and I find the discipline to avoid excessive distraction, you will find an amateur athlete's perspective on how to successfully complete an Ironman and what the process can do for you.

I'm sorry to muddy the waters of "why" with all of that medical disclaimer "junk" at the end, but I do not want anyone to get seriously hurt. If you: listen to your doctor; get off the any drugs you might be taking; properly observe rest days; and, get plenty of sleep, you should have this thing down. It will cost a lot of time and money, but if you continue to honor the Lord Jesus Christ first and your family second during this trying process, it will be worth it in the end. A parting thought. This training is very difficult and takes a lot of resources - don't short change your family. Keep the Lord Jesus Christ first and do not neglect the blessings the Lord has given you.
