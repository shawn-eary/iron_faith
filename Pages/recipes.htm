Breakfast Cereal Alternative (Beta)

There is a controversy out there on whether or not natural unprocessed oatmeal is good or bad for you, so you will have to take this recipe with a teaspoon of salt (bad "pun" intended), but I would argue that it much more athlete friendly and healthy than Wheaties and "may" have a lower Glycemic Index.. Also, I feel it may be naturally nutrient dense for those that don't want to consume tons of synthetic vitamins. It should also have very little unnatural sugars. Nothing in this recipe should be excessively processed.

Total Guestimated Time to Make: 40 Minutes

Ingredients

  1. 1/2 Cup Bob's Red Mill Organic Steel Cut Oats
  2. 1/2 Cup Juice from Walmart Canned Salmon
    (Just the juice - Bake the Salmon to eat separately or later. You can put Salmon in this concoction if you really want, but don't go overboard as you will probably enjoy the salmon better separately)
  3. 1/2 Cup Filtered Water
  4. 1 teaspoon Pink Himalayan Salt
  5. 1/2 cup chopped onions
  6. 1 teaspoon chopped garlic
  7. 1 teaspoon chopped ginger
  8. 3/4 cup raisins
  9. 2 tbsp Wheat Germ
  10. 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  11. 1/2 tbsp grass-fed butter
    (Keep away from the stove since this will be mixed in later at lower heat. Also, DO NOT USE margarine or Country Crock!!! [30][31])
  12. 3/4 sliced banana
  13. No More than - 1 tbsp Bragg Organic Apple Cider Vinegar (optional to taste)
    For safety reasons, do not add more than 1 tbsp of apple cider vinegar even if you like the taste

Steps

  1. Combine Water, Salmon Juice, Steel Cut Oats and Himalayan Salt in 4qt pot on high heat. Heat until boil, then reduce to simmer.
  2. Mix in onions and ginger while oatmeal is simmering
  3. Wait until oatmeal starts to thicken and liquid first starts to disappear. Stir occasionally.
  4. Diligently mix in the now softened butter
  5. When the oatmeal becomes just slightly more liquidy than you normally like, start mixing in the garlic and raisins
  6. Begin mixing in the Wheat Germ until the oatmeal is just barely more liquidly than you like. The oatmeal will start to thicken and rapidly approach your desired consistency
  7. Remove heat
  8. Add in optional Apple Cider Vinegar
  9. Mix in cinnamon and sliced banana.
  10. Wait for oatmeal to cool down before eating
    If you are like me you can eat it right out of the pot (ha ha!!!)

NOTEs:
  1. On the recipe above, Steel Cut is very important and also you should not get instant. Steel Cut oats take longer to prepare, but that is a good thing as it may lower the Glycemic Index which should allow the food to burn slower throughout your endurance event. Also, the steel cut oats should be safer to consume frequently since the lower perceived Glycemic Index will hopefully reduce the amount of insulin that becomes stored in your body. Please understand that while there are probably other breakfast foods like pasture raised chicken eggs that might offer and even lower Glycemic Index and even better nutrition benefits, I am pitching this particular recipe as an alternative to the "poisonous" cereals you often find in a typical grocery store.
  2. For reasons I don't want to get into right now, it's very important that you use filtered or drinking quality water in this and all foods you consume. You don't need added sugars minerals or vitamins, but you need to get the fluoride and other chemicals out of your drinking water because they can impede your athletic performance. You should exercise proper oral hygiene after consuming this food (especially if you added the apple cider vinegar) but you do not want chemicals like fluoride in your drinking water. Fluoride goes on your teeth not in your stomach...
  3. The purpose of the optional apple cider vinegar is to potentially further lower the glycemic index of this food [28]. You may not like the taste that the apple cider vinegar brings but the oatmeal mixture may make the apple cider vinegar more palatable than if you put it straight in your morning water or other athlete friendly beverage.

    WARNING: Soda Pop, Most Juices and Gatorade are not what I consider athlete family beverages. Most people should avoid juices and Gatorade altogether. Also, athletes should only consume Gatorade / juice at strategic times on event days. A few slight exceptions to this is are organic beet juice and POM branded pomegranate juice. Both of these juices have benefits that can sometimes outweigh the negative impacts they have on insulin sensitivity. If used responsibly, beet juice and POM can significantly increase your health and athletic performance. You might think that V8 vegetable juice is also a healthy option, but it's probably better to just eat the constituent vegetables raw in their un-juiced form.
    Warning and Disclaimer: Please understand that apple cider vinegar can potentially corrode your teeth [29] and/or cause other health issues if used improperly.

    Make sure you use proper dental technique after consuming the above oatmeal concoction with the apple cider vinegar added. I cannot currently recommend a good technique for neutralizing the apple cider vinegar at this time since you may need to refuel again in a few hours depending on your situation, but you need to be mindful of your teeth. If you don't plan on fueling with beet juice soon you may be able to neutralize some of the apple cider vinegar by swishing with unsweetened green tea or a (baking soda / water) mixture. If you use the baking soda / water mixture, remember to spit it out and not swallow. If all of this makes you nervous, you can leave the apple cider vinegar out but you still need to practice proper oral hygiene.
